21 Sewage Treatment Plants in Delhi Fail to Meet Standards

Date: October 2024

Location: New Delhi

Flooded streets in Gurgaon

Report Summary:
Out of the 37 sewage treatment plants (STPs) in Delhi, 21 failed to meet essential standards in several water quality parameters during August, according to a report by the Delhi Pollution Control Committee (DPCC). The STPs, which were tested for key metrics like faecal coliform, biochemical oxygen demand (BOD), total suspended solids (TSS), oil and grease, and dissolved phosphates, showed that 56% were unable to meet the required benchmarks. This continues the trend observed in July and June.

The treated or untreated water from these STPs often ends up in the Yamuna and is used for horticulture, further raising concerns about water safety and pollution. Key areas where the STPs underperformed were in TSS, BOD, COD, and faecal coliform levels. The BOD parameter indicates how much oxygen is needed for the water to naturally decompose organic matter, while faecal coliform levels indicate the presence of untreated sewage.

Impact on the Environment:

The continuous failure of the sewage treatment plants to meet environmental standards exacerbates the pollution problem in the Yamuna River and impacts public health. Efforts to improve the quality of the treated water must be prioritized to ensure a safer and cleaner environment for the city.


Severe Floods in Gurgaon Cause Major Disruptions

Date: October 2024

Location: Gurgaon

Flooded streets in Gurgaon

Report Summary:
Heavy rainfall in Gurgaon has led to severe flooding in several areas of the city. Roads and residential areas are submerged, disrupting traffic and daily activities. The Municipal Corporation of Gurgaon has reported significant damage to infrastructure, and emergency services are working to rescue residents trapped in waterlogged areas.

The weather department has issued further warnings for more rainfall in the coming days, raising concerns about worsening conditions. Many residents are facing power outages, and schools and offices have been closed as a precautionary measure.

Impact on the Community:

The flooding has disrupted transport services, including metro operations and roadways, making it difficult for people to commute. Authorities are urging citizens to stay indoors and avoid unnecessary travel.


Bangalore Faces Massive Flooding After Heavy Rains

Date: October 2024

Location: Bangalore

Flooded areas in Bangalore after heavy rains

Report Summary:
Bangalore has been hit by heavy rainfall over the last few days, leading to massive flooding in various parts of the city. The water has engulfed residential areas and streets, bringing the city's infrastructure to a standstill. The local government has declared a state of emergency and is coordinating rescue operations.

Due to the flooding, several parts of the city are without electricity, and authorities are working to restore power. Public transport has been severely affected, with buses and other vehicles struggling to move through waterlogged roads.

Impact on the City:

The flooding has caused widespread disruption in daily life. Schools, businesses, and IT parks are temporarily closed, and many residents have been evacuated to safer locations. Authorities are advising people to avoid travel and stay indoors as the water levels continue to rise.
